SEARCH and SORT

 

Adjacency

Enter a search for a phrase in quotation marks. If no match is found, the system will not automatically search this as individual words.

If no quotation marks are used,

  • in a search sorted by Relevancy, multiple words are searched as words and a phrase.
  • In a title or date sorted search, words entered are searched as a phrase. Only if no match is found, will the search automatically be repeated inserting the boolean operator AND between the words.

Examples:

  • "Medical care"
  • "Cold war"
  • planet jupiter
  • "series in computer graphics"
Boolean operators

 

To search multiple words not as a phrase use:

  • AND to find all of the words in any order
  • OR to find any of the words
  • AND NOT to exclude one or more words

When using more than one operator, use parentheses to group words.

To search operators as words, enter them in quotation marks.

Synonyms: & replaces AND ; | replaces OR ; &| replaces AND NOT

 

Examples:

  • law and youth
  • polar and not bear
  • (capital punishment or death penalty) and Canada
  • art and (mexico and not new)
  • War "and" peace, or "War and peace"
  • (art | painting) & (mexico &! new)
Proximity operators

Refine your search by using:

  • NEAR to specify words within 10 words of each other, in any order,
  • WITHIN # to specify words which occur within a set range of each other,
  • BEFORE to specify that a word must precede another,
  • AFTER to indicate that a word must follow another.

To search operators as words, enter them in quotation marks.

Synonym: ~ replaces NEAR

 

 

Examples:

  • women near men
  • canada within 11 peace-keeping operations
  • sunday before school
  • "Near" east, or "Near east"
  • "Before" time began, or "Before time began"
Truncation or Wildcarding

 

Words may be internally or right-hand truncated by using:

  • ? for one character within a word,
  • * (single asterisk) for zero to five characters,
  • ** (double asterisks) for any number of characters.
  • NOTE: * or ** wildcarding can only be used after at least two characters.

 

Examples:

  • wom*n and child*
  • ontario near econom*
  • teen** and mass media
  • Sven Anders*n
  • lab*r
Common words and initial articles

 

All words, including one and two letter words and initial articles, are indexed and should be included in a search.

Initial articles (a, an, the, le, la, les) are ignored only if they are the first word in a search, but will be included if enclosed in quotation marks,

 

 

Examples:

  • Gone with the wind
  • "Le Blanc" or "Le" Blanc
  • b flat
  • "a" flat, or "a flat"
Field specifiers

 

Specify fields to search, using the following field abbreviations:

  • a: (for author field),
  • t: (for title field),
  • s: (for subject heading field)
  • n: (for notes field)

Multiple words will only be searched as a phrase. To search as words repeat the field abbreviation.

 

 

Examples:

  • t: Greenhouse gas and a: Canada
  • a: shakespeare and t: macbeth and recording
  • a: canada and a: industry and s: indust**
  • a: asimov and s: astronomy

 

LIMITING A KEYWORD SEARCH

To perform ONLY a search using the limits without entering any keywords, enter ** in the keyword box, pick or enter a limit and click on Search

Search and Sort

 

Three sort options are available:

  • Date (default) : sorts in reverse order of date of publication, i.e. latest date first
  • Alphabetical: by title
  • Relevance: Relevancy of a record is indicated by a one to five star display. Ranking occurs on the fields that include the search term. The title field is most relevant, followed by author, subject and notes. All records with search terms in the same field are ranked according to the position of those terms and a variety of other factors.
